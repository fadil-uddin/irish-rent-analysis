# Irish Rental Affordability Analysis
## Trends, Regional Differences, and Affordability Pressure

---

# 1. Objective

Analyse Irish rental market data to understand:

- how rents changed over time
- which counties have the highest latest rents
- where rent growth has been fastest
- how Dublin compares with the rest of Ireland

---

# 2. Dataset

Source: RTB Average Monthly Rent Report via CSO PxStat

Scope:

- 26 Irish counties
- Quarterly data
- 2007Q4 to 2025Q4
- All bedrooms
- All property types
- Average monthly rent in EUR

---

# 3. National Rent Trend

Key finding:

Average county-level monthly rent increased from EUR 778 in 2007Q4 to EUR 1,336 in 2025Q4.

This is an increase of EUR 558, or 71.8%.

# 4. Dublin vs Non-Dublin

Key finding:

Dublin remains the highest-rent market.

In 2025Q4, Dublin average rent was EUR 2,165.

The non-Dublin county average was approximately EUR 1,302.

Dublin rents were about 66.2% higher than the non-Dublin average.

# 6. Fastest Rent Growth

Fastest rent growth since 2007Q4:

- Cavan: 91.9%
- Longford: 91.3%
- Galway: 89.6%
- Leitrim: 86.0%
- Waterford: 84.1%

Interpretation:

Affordability pressure is not only a Dublin issue. Several non-Dublin counties show very strong percentage growth.

# 7. Recommendations and Limitations

Practical implications:

- Track both absolute rent levels and percentage growth.
- Monitor fast-growing counties even when rents remain below Dublin.
- Combine rent data with income, inflation, and housing supply data in future analysis.

Limitations:

- Uses registered tenancy data.
- County-level only.
- Does not include income data.
- Not inflation-adjusted.
- Descriptive analysis only; no causality claimed.
